Small-Scale UAV Theory and Practice - ECE 163

This course taught us about the mathmatical models involved in tracking the full state of our aircraft model as well implementing control theory for autonomous flight. The mathematical tools utilized include coordinate frames, aerodynamics, equations of motion, full non-linear simulation, linearized dynamics models and trim states, force and moment balances for steady flight, flight controls by successive loop closure, state space control, path planning and guidance, sensors and estimation.

For my team's final project in this class, we chose to implement a camera on the bottom of the simulated UAV. This camera would start out in a "Searching for Target" state while moving towards the last known location of the target. Once the target was calculated to be within the visible range of the camera, the location was known and the UAV would enter a "Circle/Following" state where it would continue tracking the target.
